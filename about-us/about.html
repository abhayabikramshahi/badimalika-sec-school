About Us

About Our School

Badimalika Secondary School is a public academic institution located in Syuna, Raskot, Kalikot, Karnali Province, Nepal. It is affiliated with the National Examinations Board (NEB) and the Council for Technical Education and Vocational Training (CTEVT). The school offers Ten Plus Two (10+2) programs in Humanities, Management, Education, and Agriculture streams. It also provides vocational training programs like JTA (Junior Technical Assistant) in Plant Science, which admits 40 students each year. The medium of instruction is both English and Nepali, ensuring accessibility for local and national students.

The school aims to provide affordable education with a focus on quality learning and practical skills. It offers scholarship opportunities based on merit and quota systems to support underprivileged students. Students seeking admission to the Ten Plus Two programs must have completed their Secondary Education Examination (SEE) with a minimum GPA of 1.6.

In addition to its academic offerings, Badimalika Secondary School maintains a student-friendly environment with facilities that support technical, vocational, and academic growth. The school is dedicated to fostering an inclusive learning atmosphere that empowers students to achieve academic success and develop skills relevant to their future careers.
